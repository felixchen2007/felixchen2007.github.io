About

Curious about systems that actually have to work.

I like engineering work that is hands-on: wiring something, testing it, realizing it does not behave the way I expected, then tracing the issue until the design finally makes sense.

My interest in electrical engineering grew from projects involving Arduino systems, circuit prototyping, soldering, wiring, and small-scale hardware builds. I enjoy the moment where a design stops being only a diagram and starts becoming a real physical system with constraints, noise, failure points, and tradeoffs.

I am especially interested in embedded systems, electronics, PCB design, and engineering projects that combine software logic with physical behavior. This portfolio collects the projects, build notes, and technical work that shaped those interests.

Current Focus

  • Learning more electrical and computer engineering fundamentals
  • Building cleaner Arduino and embedded systems projects
  • Designing more custom PCB and circuit-based projects
  • Improving technical communication through reports, diagrams, and build logs

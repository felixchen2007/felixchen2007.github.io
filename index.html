Arduino Claw Project

APSC 101 · 2026

Built and programmed an Arduino-controlled claw system using electrical components, wiring, and microcontroller logic. The goal was to create a mechanism that could respond to programmed inputs and perform a physical task through controlled motion.

My main role was connecting the Arduino to different components, organizing the wiring, debugging input/output behavior, and adjusting the code so the system responded in the intended way. During testing, I learned how even a small wiring issue or logic error could change the behavior of the entire system.

This project helped me become more interested in embedded systems because it showed how software, electrical connections, and mechanical behavior all depend on each other in a real design.

Battery-Powered Vehicle Electrical System

Kwantlen Science Challenge

Worked on the electrical side of a battery-powered vehicle designed for a local STEM competition. The vehicle had to move across a course while carrying its electrical and mechanical components reliably under load.

My role included connecting components, organizing wires, soldering connections, and helping make sure the electrical system stayed functional while the vehicle moved. Although our vehicle did not perform as well as we hoped, the project still had a strong impact on me.

Seeing cleaner and faster vehicles from other teams made me want to understand what made their designs better. I became more aware of the importance of clean wiring, efficient power delivery, mechanical-electrical integration, and reliable construction. This experience became one of the reasons I became interested in electrical engineering.

Rainwater Harvesting System Model

APSC 101 · Engineering Design

Developed an engineering model for a rainwater harvesting system designed for a remote two-person household. The project involved estimating household water demand, using rainfall data, evaluating system performance, and comparing design options through stakeholder criteria.

The technical work included Excel modeling, pump and system curve reasoning, flow calculations, risk assessment, and a weighted decision matrix. The design also required balancing technical performance with cost, maintainability, stakeholder satisfaction, and long-term reliability.

This project helped me practice systems thinking because the final design was not based on one calculation alone. It depended on how different engineering, environmental, and stakeholder factors affected each other.
